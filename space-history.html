The era we're in

New Space, explained

New Space is the shift, from roughly the 2000s onward, from spaceflight run almost entirely by governments to one driven by private companies, private capital and commercial markets. It is less a single event than a change in culture and economics: reusable rockets, mass-produced satellites, falling launch costs, and agencies buying services instead of building everything themselves.

      What defines the era

      The turning point most people point to is the Ansari X Prize in 2004 — the first privately built crewed spacecraft — and the founding of SpaceX (2002) and Blue Origin (2000). When SpaceX landed and reflew an orbital rocket booster (2015–2017), the economics of access to space changed for good.

GNSS positioning

High Kp and ionospheric storms add range error and can cause loss of lock — relevant for survey, precision agriculture and any GNSS-dependent timing.

HF & aviation radio

M/X-class flares ionise the dayside D-layer, causing shortwave fade-outs on the sunlit side of the Earth — including South America when the flare is in local daytime.

Satellite operations

Storms heat and expand the upper atmosphere, raising drag on LEO objects and shifting orbits — directly relevant to SSA tracking and conjunction screening.

Observing at OALM

Space weather doesn't cloud the sky, but strong geomagnetic activity raises sky brightness and, at extreme storms, faint aurora can reach unusually low southern latitudes.
